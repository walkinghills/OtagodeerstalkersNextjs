Club Hunts

Organised hunting trips for Otago Branch members — a great way to get into the field with experienced hunters.

Annual Hunts

The branch organises at least two main hunts per year, with additional opportunities as interest and conditions allow.

🦌

Deer Hunt

One of the two main annual club hunts. Red deer are the primary quarry, with New Zealand's South Island backcountry providing excellent habitat.

  • ✓Annual event for branch members
  • ✓Experienced members available to guide newer hunters
  • ✓Trophy entries welcomed for the annual competition

Typically held during the roar (March–April). Exact dates confirmed at the preceding club meeting.

🐐

Goat Hunt

The second of the two main annual club hunts. Goat hunting is a great entry point for newer members — good numbers of animals and accessible terrain.

  • ✓Annual event, generally earlier in the year
  • ✓Good for introducing newer hunters to the field
  • ✓Trophies eligible for the annual competition

Typically held earlier in the year (February–March). Good for newer members — accessible terrain and welcoming atmosphere.

🏔

Tahr & Wallaby Hunts

Additional hunts for Himalayan tahr and wallaby are organised as interest and opportunity permit — typically subject to access availability and member demand.

  • ✓Organised as interest arises
  • ✓Express interest at a club meeting

What to Bring on a Club Hunt

New to club hunts? Here's what experienced Otago members typically pack.

Essentials

  • ✓Current membership card
  • ✓Firearms licence
  • ✓Appropriate calibre rifle and ammunition
  • ✓Ear and eye protection
  • ✓PLB (Personal Locator Beacon) — mandatory in backcountry

Gear & Clothing

  • ✓Layered merino or synthetic base layers
  • ✓Waterproof outer shell
  • ✓Sturdy boots (broken-in)
  • ✓Pack with overnight capability
  • ✓First aid kit and bivvy bag

For Game Recovery

  • ✓Knife and whetstone
  • ✓Game bags
  • ✓Rope or pack frame for carry-out
  • ✓Camera for trophy photos

For DOC hunting permits and area information: doc.govt.nz/hunting

Inter-Branch Events

Hunting with Other Branches

The Otago Branch regularly connects with other NZDA branches throughout New Zealand — both hosting visiting groups and attending hunts organised by other branches.

These events are a great opportunity to explore new country and build connections with the wider NZDA hunting community.

  • ✓Reciprocal hosting arrangements with other NZDA branches
  • ✓Access to hunting areas outside Otago
  • ✓Dates and details announced at club meetings
🌿

How to Get Involved

1

Become a Member

Club hunts are open to financial Otago Branch members. If you're not yet a member, joining is straightforward through the NZDA national website.

Join Now →
2

Come to a Meeting

Upcoming hunts are announced and planned at monthly club meetings. The 2nd Monday of every month at 7:30pm at 53 Malvern Street, Woodhaugh, Dunedin.

3

Register Your Interest

Let club members or the committee know which hunts you're interested in. Numbers are sometimes limited, so expressing early interest is worthwhile.
