HUNTS Course

Hunter National Training Scheme — New Zealand's nationally recognised programme for safe and responsible hunting, run by NZDA in partnership with Fish & Game NZ.

What is the HUNTS Course?

HUNTS (Hunter National Training Scheme) was established in 1987 and has since trained thousands of New Zealand hunters in safe, ethical, and competent hunting practice. It is administered by the NZDA in partnership with Fish & Game NZ.

The programme is designed primarily for people new to hunting, covering everything from gear selection and bush navigation to game identification, firearms safety, and emergency preparedness — both in the classroom and out in the field.

  • 16 hours of theory instruction
  • 4 field days including at least 1 overnight camping trip
  • Nationally recognised qualification
  • Run by experienced NZDA instructors
To register: Contact Frans Laas on 027 230 7157

What You'll Learn

The HUNTS programme covers the full range of skills needed to hunt safely and effectively in New Zealand's backcountry.

Preparation & Equipment

  • Clothing selection for backcountry conditions
  • Gear and pack selection
  • Emergency preparedness and first aid

Bush Skills & Safety

  • Bush travel and navigation
  • River safety and crossings
  • Weather awareness and decision-making

Hunting Practice

  • Game species identification
  • Hunting methods and ethics
  • Meat and trophy preservation

Firearms & Shooting

  • Firearms safety for hunting
  • Shooting techniques and positioning
  • Firearm setup and sighting

Who Should Attend?

Eligibility

  • Primarily aimed at new hunters aged 18 and over
  • Younger hunters are welcome with guardian approval
  • A firearms licence is preferred, but not required to enrol
  • Adequate fitness for backcountry pack carrying is required

Commitment Required

Places on the course are limited. Participants are expected to attend all stages — theory sessions and all field days.
  • All theory sessions (16 hours total)
  • All 4 field days
  • At least 1 overnight camping trip is included

Course Schedule

Courses run approximately once per year, subject to demand. The next course date will be confirmed at club meetings or directly by the coordinator.
  • 16 hours of theory — split across evening sessions
  • 4 field days — weekends, including at least one overnight
  • Typical duration: 4–6 weeks from first session to completion
  • Places are limited — register early to secure a spot
